Food & Accommodations

Discover Sibulan’s iconic dishes and comfortable places to stay.

Local Foods

Budbud Kabog & Native Kakanin

In Sibulan, local pasalubong shops often offer budbud kabog, sticky millet-based rice cakes wrapped in banana leaves. It’s a favorite among locals and visitors alike, especially for early morning snacks or as take-home treats for friends and family.

Grilled Seafood & Coastal Dishes

Thanks to its seaside location, Sibulan is known for fresh seafood dishes sold by local vendors, grilled fish, squid, and shellfish cooked over charcoal. Visitors can enjoy a simple, flavorful meal with ocean breeze and a laid-back coastal vibe.

Native Delicacies & Sweet Snacks

At Sibulan’s town market and small eateries, you can find traditional sweets and snacks, native cakes, rice-based desserts, and other local treats. Perfect for those who want to taste authentic provincial flavors while exploring the town.

Accommodations

Sea Forest Resort

Located by the coast, Sea Forest Resort offers cottages and rooms, ideal for beach lovers and families. Guests can enjoy ocean views, swimming pools, and easy access to the sea, making it a top choice for a relaxing stay when visiting Sibulan’s attractions.

Airport-Side Hotel / Inn

For travelers arriving via the nearby airport, a comfortable hotel or inn near Dumaguete–Sibulan Airport offers convenience, clean rooms, and easy access to transport, making it a practical base for exploring the town and nearby islands.

Local Homestays & Guesthouses

Budget-friendly guesthouses and homestays in the town proper offer a simple but welcoming stay. Great for students, budget travelers, or those wanting to experience everyday life in Sibulan, often close to markets, transport hubs, and municipal centers.
